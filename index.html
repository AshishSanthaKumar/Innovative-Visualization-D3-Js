SOVIET SPACE DOGS (1950 - 1966)

This Visualization describes the dogs who were part of the soviet space program from 1950 to 1966. The following attibutes are described in this Visualization.

  • Gender: This is a categorical variable. The Marks are the points which represent each dog. The Channels are color and the horizontal position. The left half of the vertical line which is also pink in color represents the female dogs. The right half of the vertical line, which is also blue in color represents the Male dogs

  • Altitude: This is a quantitative variable. The Marks for this one is points too. The Channels is the vertical position of this points. The point in the lower end have gone to a lower altitude than the ones at the top. The Interaction is the tooltip which also gives the name and the altitude of the dog.

  • Fate: This is a categorical variable. The Marks are the lines on the face of the smiley. The Channels is the Angle/tilt on the mouth line of the smiley. The Interaction is linked with the data points on the rocket. As the user hovers over the data points, the fate of that dog is displayed as the change in smiley and a label

  • Additional Information: When the user hovers over the data points, along with the fate, the year of the last mission the dog was on is displayed
